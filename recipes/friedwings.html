Fried Chicken Wings

An American Dish with Chicken Wings and Hot Buffalo Sauce

The dish calls for chicken wings, flour, and many spices. Season your 12 wings with seasoned salt,and mix flour, salt, black pepper, cayenne pepper, and paprika together in big bowl. Place the wings in the mixture and seperate on a plate so they don't touch, after that refrigerate for 15 to 30 minutes.

Drenge the wings in the mixture again and once again refrigerate for 15 to 30 minutes.Heat some oil in a deep fryer or saucepan to 375 degrees F. Fry the wings for 9 to 12 minutes, or they are no longer pink at the bone. Transfer fried wings to a large stainless steel bowl and drizzle sauce over the wings and toss to coat.

Ingredients

  • 12 small chicken wings
  • ¼ teaspoon seasoned salt, or to taste
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon coarse salt
  • ½ teaspoon ground black pepper
  • ¼ teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • ¼ teaspoon paprika
  • 1 (12 fluid ounce) bottle Buffalo wing sauce (such as Frank's®), or to taste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • ⅛ teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ⅛ teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 2 quarts vegetable oil for frying

Steps

  1. Season chicken wings lightly with seasoned salt.
  2. Mix flour, salt, black pepper, cayenne pepper, and paprika together in a wide, shallow bowl and then press wings into flour mixture to coat and arrange onto a large plate so they do not touch.
  3. Refrigerate coated wings for 15 to 30 minutes.
  4. Dredge wings again in flour mixture and return to the plate. Refrigerate wings again 15 to 30 minutes.
  5. Heat oil in a deep-fryer or large saucepan to 375 degrees F.
  6. Fry chicken wings in hot oil until crisp and no longer pink at the bone and the juices run clear, 9 to 12 minutes. An instant-read thermometer inserted into the thickest part of the meat, near the bone should read 165 degrees F.
  7. Transfer fried wings to a large stainless steel bowl and drizzle sauce over the wings and toss to coat.
